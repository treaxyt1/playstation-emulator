God of War 2: Remove Green Screen Glitch on PCSX2

Fix the annoying semi-transparent green overlay and enjoy GOW2 in crystal clear quality.

One of the most persistent visual issues in God of War 2 on PCSX2 is the "Green Screen Glitch." This bug covers the screen in a semi-transparent green tint, making it almost impossible to appreciate the game's stunning art style. This happens primarily on low-end hardware or when using specific hardware renderers.

Why is the Screen Green?

This issue is usually caused by the way the emulator handles "Depth of Field" or "Fog" effects in the original PS2 code. On certain GPUs (especially integrated ones), these effects aren't rendered correctly, resulting in the green overlay. It can also be triggered by using "Hardware Hacks" without the correct offsets.

The most effective way to remove the green screen is using the skipdraw hack:

  • Go to Config > Graphics Settings.
  • Navigate to the Hacks tab (Enable Manual Hardware Renderer Fixes first).
  • Find Skipdraw Range.
  • Set both values to 1 (or set Skipdraw to 1-3 depending on your version).
  • This will "skip" the rendering of the broken frame buffers causing the green tint.

Alternative Fixes for Low End PCs

If skipdraw causes other visual issues (like disappearing menus), try these alternatives:

1. Half-Pixel Offset

In the same Hacks menu, find Half-Pixel Offset. Set it to Special (Texture). This can sometimes align the malfunctioning buffers correctly without needing to skip them entirely.

2. Use Vulkan Renderer

The Vulkan API handles PS2 effects much more accurately than OpenGL or Direct3D 11 on modern low-end hardware. Switching your Renderer to Vulkan in the Graphics tab often resolves the green tint automatically.

3. Blending Accuracy

Switch your Blending Accuracy to High or Full. While this is more taxing on the GPU, it is the most accurate way to fix the glitch without using hacks. If your PC can handle it, this is the preferred method.
