God of War 2 PCSX2 Best Settings for Intel HD 620

Unlock 60FPS on integrated graphics with this definitive optimization guide.

Running a masterpiece like God of War 2 on an integrated GPU like the Intel HD 620 might seem like an impossible task. However, with the latest versions of PCSX2 and the right configuration, you can achieve a stable, playable experience that rivals the original hardware.

Why Intel HD 620 Struggles

The Intel HD 620 is a mobile integrated graphics chip found in many laptops. It lacks dedicated VRAM, relying instead on your system's RAM. God of War 2 is one of the most demanding titles for the PS2, pushing the emulator's GS (Graphics Synthesizer) thread to its limits.

  • EE/VOPs: Speedhacks enabled. Set EE Cycle Rate to -1 or -2.
  • V01/V02: Set to Multi-Threaded microVU1 (MTVU). This is critical for dual-core CPUs.
  • Renderer: Direct3D 11 (Hardware) or Vulkan. Vulkan generally performs better on newer Intel drivers.

Optimization Walkthrough

To get the most out of your Intel HD 620, follow these specific steps in the PCSX2 Graphics Plugin Settings:

1. Internal Resolution

Keep the Internal Resolution at Native (PS2). While 720p (2x Native) looks better, the Intel HD 620 will likely drop frames in intense combat scenes. If you have 8GB of dual-channel RAM, you might squeeze out 720p, but for competitive 60FPS, Native is king.

2. Textures and Filtering

  • Texture Filtering: Bilinear (PS2). Avoid "Forced" settings.
  • Anisotropic Filtering: Off. This saves significant bandwidth on integrated graphics.
  • Mipmapping: Basic (Fast).

3. Accuracy Blending

Set Accuracy Blending to Basic or None. While this might cause minor visual artifacts (like shadows looking slightly off), it provides a massive boost to the framerate.
